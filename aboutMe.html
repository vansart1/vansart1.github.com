About Me:

       Hi I'm Victor. I'm an engineer, sailor, tinkerer, and inventor. When I'm not out on the water sailing, doing schoolwork, or hanging out with friends, you'll find me working on projects that I find interesting.

       Ever since I was I kid, I was always curious and loved to take things apart to see how they worked inside. That interest has still persisted to this day and my curiosity has not died down one bit. In high school I completed some very cool projects where I converted a wankel rotary engine to run on propane and also created light reactive nanocrystals with a microwave.

       My interests further blossomed when I got to college and started studying electrical engineering. As I learned more and more about circuits, I created a game similar to Space Invaders using basic digital chips, a radio transmitter to play IPhone music over the radio, and a swarmbot used for simulated chemical detection.

       Most recently, at my summer internship, I created a power meter that outputed its results to a website. Currently my project is to automate my bedroom to make day to day activities easier! I am currently a senior undergraduate studying Electrical Engineering at Tufts University near Boston, MA.
